What Webull actually is

Webull is a self-directed brokerage: commission-free stocks, ETFs, and options, plus IRAs, crypto (via a linked Webull Pay app), and futures. Unlike Acorns, nothing is automated — you pick every trade. Unlike Robinhood, the platform assumes you already know roughly what you're doing: real-time charting, dozens of technical indicators, stock screeners, and a genuinely excellent paper-trading sandbox for testing strategies with fake money before risking real cash.

⚠ How Webull actually makes money

Commission-free doesn't mean free to run. Webull's own pricing page is refreshingly honest about this: revenue comes from stock loans, interest on uninvested cash, margin interest, and payment for order flow — the same mix every zero-commission broker uses. Payment for order flow means your trade orders are routed to market makers who pay Webull for the flow, which can mean slightly worse execution prices than a broker charging a flat commission with premium routing. For most retail order sizes this difference is negligible, but it's not literally free — it's a different pricing model, not the absence of one.

The real cost: leaving

The one fee that actually stings is the $75 ACATS transfer-out charge if you ever move your portfolio to another brokerage. It's a standard industry fee (many brokers charge similarly), but worth knowing before you deposit — moving a modest account elsewhere later costs real money. Selling everything to cash first avoids the transfer fee but can trigger capital gains taxes in a taxable account, so it's not a clean workaround.

✓ What Webull does better than most free brokers

Unlimited free paper trading with both stocks and options is genuinely rare at this level of quality — it's a real sandbox for testing a strategy before committing actual money, not a stripped-down demo. Combined with a stock screener and real charting tools, Webull is one of the strongest free packages available to US retail traders who want to actually learn technical analysis rather than just tap buy.

FAQ

Is Webull really commission-free?

Yes for stocks, ETFs, and equity options. Certain index options carry a small per-contract fee, and standard regulatory fees apply to all trades regardless of broker.

Does Webull charge a fee to transfer out?

Yes — $75 per ACATS transfer to another brokerage. Standard ACH withdrawals are free.

Is Webull good for beginners?

Better for people who've outgrown Robinhood's simplicity. The tools are powerful but the interface and educational content are less beginner-friendly.

Webull vs Robinhood: what's the real difference?

Both are commission-free with similar base costs. Webull wins on charting, screeners, and paper trading. Robinhood wins on simplicity and its IRA match.
