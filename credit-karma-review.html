What Credit Karma Actually Is

Credit Karma is a free credit-monitoring platform: real VantageScore 3.0 credit scores from TransUnion and Equifax, full credit reports from both bureaus, and real-time alerts when something on your report changes — a new account opened, a hard inquiry, a missed payment reported, your utilization shifting. No monthly fee, no credit card on file, no free trial that turns into a charge later. It's been free since it launched in 2007, and stayed free after Intuit (TurboTax's parent company) bought it in 2020 for $8.1 billion.

On top of monitoring, Credit Karma runs a recommendation engine: based on your score and credit profile, it suggests credit cards and loans you're likely to qualify for. That's also where the business model lives — more on that below.

How Credit Karma actually makes moneyNot by charging you — by referral commissions. When you apply for a credit card or loan through one of Credit Karma's recommendations and get approved, the lender pays Credit Karma a fee. That's why every page nudges you toward a product: the recommendation engine is the revenue engine. It's not a scam and nothing is hidden from you at signup, but it's worth knowing that "personalized offer" also means "the offer Credit Karma gets paid to show you."

The One Caveat Most Reviews Skip: It's Not a FICO Score

Credit Karma's score is a real, accurate VantageScore 3.0 — not a fake "educational" number some free tools use. But most lenders — mortgage, auto, and most credit card issuers — actually pull a FICO Score when they check you, which uses a different scoring model. It's common to see your Credit Karma VantageScore sit 20-40 points off from the FICO Score a lender pulls, in either direction. Treat Credit Karma as an excellent directional tracker — is your credit trending up or down, and why — not as the exact number that will show up on a loan application.

Free Tax Filing: The Nuance Worth Knowing

Credit Karma still advertises "Free Credit Karma Tax" on its site, but it's not the same product it used to be. The original standalone Credit Karma Tax was sold off in 2020 as part of the Intuit acquisition (regulators required it) and became Cash App Taxes — a completely separate company now. What Credit Karma calls "Free Credit Karma Tax" today is a TurboTax-powered offer, since TurboTax and Credit Karma are both owned by Intuit. Simple returns — about 37% of filers, per Intuit's own numbers — file completely free. More complex returns (itemized deductions, self-employment income, multiple schedules) require a paid TurboTax tier. Check whether your return actually qualifies for the free tier before counting on it.

Pros and Cons

  • Full credit reports, not just a score
  • Real-time alerts on new accounts, inquiries, and score changes

Where it costs you attention

  • "Free Credit Karma Tax" only covers simple returns; complex returns need paid TurboTax

Who Should Use Credit Karma — and Who Shouldn't

Get it if:

You want to actually see your credit report and score without paying for the privilege. You're working on improving your credit and want to track progress over time. You want alerts the moment something changes — a new account, a hard inquiry, a reported late payment.

Skip it — or supplement it — if:

You're about to apply for a mortgage or major loan and need the exact FICO Score a lender will actually pull — check with the lender directly or use a FICO-specific service instead. You're shopping for a credit card or loan and want a neutral comparison, not one weighted toward what pays Credit Karma the most.

FAQ

Is Credit Karma actually free?
Yes — no monthly fee, no credit card required to sign up, no trial that flips to a paid plan. It makes money through referral commissions when you apply for a recommended card or loan, not by charging you.
Does Credit Karma show my real credit score?
It shows a real, accurate VantageScore 3.0 from TransUnion and Equifax. Most lenders actually pull a FICO Score, which can land 20-40 points off. Treat it as a directional tracker, not the exact number a lender will see.
Is Credit Karma Tax still a thing?
The original Credit Karma Tax was sold off in 2020 and became Cash App Taxes, an unrelated product. "Free Credit Karma Tax" today is a TurboTax-powered offer — only simple returns (about 37% of filers) file completely free.
Will checking my score hurt my credit?
No. Credit Karma uses a soft inquiry, which never affects your credit and isn't visible to lenders. Check as often as you want.
