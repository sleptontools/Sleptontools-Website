Straight Answers

faq
How does this site make money?
Two ways: affiliate commissions when you sign up for a tool through our links (costs you nothing extra), and sales of our own Personal Finance OS template. Rankings are never sold — if a tool pays well but performs badly, it doesn't get listed.
Do I need a paid app to fix my money?
No. Rocket Money's free tier plus any consistent tracking system covers most people. Paid tools earn their fee only if they change your behavior — that's the only math that matters.
What should I start with if I'm broke right now?
Free subscription audit first (cancel the leaks), then a zero-based budget. Spend nothing on tools until the leaks are plugged.
Are the app scores on this site actually independent?
Yes. We test every tool ourselves and score it before we ever check whether an affiliate relationship is even available. A partner paying us doesn't buy a better score — we've given lower scores to tools with our own affiliate links attached when the fine print earned it.
How often do you re-check pricing?
Every article is re-verified against the provider's own pricing page before it's published or meaningfully updated, and again on a monthly rolling basis. App pricing changes constantly — a stale number is how a review site loses trust fast.
Should I use a budgeting app, an investing app, or both?
Budgeting first, always. An investing app can't fix a leaking paycheck. Once your spending is under control and you have real money left over each month, that's the point to start looking at investing or net-worth tools.
What's the difference between the apps you review and the Personal Finance OS?
The apps automate a specific task — tracking, investing, transferring. The Personal Finance OS is a manual system you actually run yourself in Notion: budget, debt payoff, savings goals, net worth, all in one place. Some people want automation, some want to see and control every number themselves. Neither is wrong.
Do you review apps you wouldn't personally use?
No. Every tool on this site was actually opened, tested, and used — not summarized from a press release or another site's review. If we wouldn't put our own money into it, we say so plainly instead of writing around it.
